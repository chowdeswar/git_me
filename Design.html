Technology Selection Advisory

At Global Reach Technologies we believe that investing in a video surveillance system is a smart move. You have many assets to protect so you need to make sure your security system does what you want it to do. Don’t run the risk of something happening and then discover the video has not been captured properly.

Selecting the right cameras for your video surveillance system is critical to the success of your system.

First, you need to determine what you want the camera to capture for you. Here are some things to consider:

  • Do you want to see a scene and know if something is happening?
  • Do you want to see an event and determine exactly what is happening?
  • Do you want to see the individual and determine exactly who is involved?

Your answers to these questions will begin to affect the type of cameras you need. However, with an ever increase number of vendors, technologies and options, it can quickly become a somewhat overwhelming task to differentiate features and functions. Our technology advisory services will help you match your specific needs to the right partner and solution.

Project Management

Complete your video surveillance project on time and within budget with the help of Global Reach Technologies’ project management service. Using proven tools and processes, we help you through all phases of the project management lifecycle. Included in our solution, is a full-time project manager who will work with your team to ensure a smooth and timely completion of your video surveillance project.

Specifically, our project management methodology includes:

  • Managing Scope of Work – Clear deliverable, with clear understanding of the associated costs.
  • Work Breakdown Structure (WBS) – Project Schedule and Responsibility Matrix Coordinates and fully defines project ownership, timing and overall responsibilities.
  • Project kickoff meeting – Establishes processes and guidelines for project execution, scope expectations, as well as clear acceptance criteria.
  • Elimination of surprises – Keeps everyone on the same page. Uses contract design reviews, milestone acceptance check points and final acceptance reviews; track project according to project plan and report status, progress and issues.
  • Project acceptance – set criteria for acceptance of completed project based on the scope, budget and schedule. Minimizes risks of cost overruns; maximize on-schedule and in-scope completeness.
  • Deliverable management – delivers all project documentation, checklists, diagrams, change orders, analysis and recommendations for future improvements via a “Lessons Learned” session
